Exploring Housing Options

Where Should You Start Your Home Search?

Starting the search for a new home can be exciting but requires careful planning and research. From considering budget constraints to exploring different locations, it's important to be thorough in your approach to finding the perfect home.

Available Resources for House Hunting

  • Browse online real estate listings for up-to-date property information.

  • Check local newspapers or online classified ads for housing deals.

  • Visit property fairs or exhibitions to explore multiple options in one place.

  • Reach out to real estate agents for professional assistance in finding homes.

  • Consult with developers for upcoming housing projects in desirable locations.

  • Keep an eye on billboards or hoardings advertising new properties.

  • Ask friends, family, or colleagues for recommendations and leads.

Choosing the Right Location

The location of your home plays a significant role in your daily life and overall satisfaction. Consider the following factors when selecting a location:

  • Proximity to work: Choose a location that minimizes your daily commute.

  • Transportation options: Ensure good access to public transportation and well-maintained roads.

  • Amenities nearby: Look for schools, markets, hospitals, and other essential services close to your prospective home.

  • Future growth potential: Evaluate the long-term value and development plans for the area to ensure a sound investment.

  • Social infrastructure: Access to parks, recreational facilities, and community services can enhance your quality of life.

Home Loans & Finance

Steps to Secure a Loan


1. Apply and Get Preliminary Evaluation
  • Submit your application along with the processing fee.
  • The bank will review your credit history and supporting documents.
2. Verification by the Bank
  • If your application is eligible, you'll be required to present your documents in person.
  • If ineligible, the processing fee is non-refundable.
  • The bank will verify your documents and may conduct home and workplace visits.
  • The property will be appraised, and your repayment capacity will be evaluated.
  • If approved, an offer letter detailing the loan terms will be issued.
3. Formal Loan Approval
  • Carefully review the offer letter and consult a legal expert if necessary.
  • Sign the loan agreement once you’re comfortable with the terms.
4. Post-Signature Steps
  • Submit post-dated cheques as required.
  • The bank will disburse the loan amount either as a lump sum (for ready properties) or in installments (for under-construction properties).

Required Documents for Loan Application

Here's a list of documents you need to prepare:

  • Loan Application Form: Available from the bank or financial institution.
  • Processing Fee: Typically between 0.25% and 0.50% of the loan amount, non-refundable.
  • Photographs: At least three recent photos.
  • Identity Proof: PAN card, driver's license, Aadhar card, or passport.
  • Address Proof: Driver's license, passport, recent utility bills, or Aadhar card.
  • Proof of Income (Salaried): Income tax returns (last three years) and recent salary slips.
  • Proof of Business (Self-Employed): Documentation proving the existence of your business.
  • Income Tax Returns (Self-Employed): Returns for the last three years along with income computation.
  • CA Certified Financial Statements (Self-Employed): Balance sheets and profit & loss accounts for the last three years.
  • Advance Tax Payments (Self-Employed): Copies of challans showing tax payments.
  • Bank Statements: Statements or passbooks from the last six months.
  • CIBIL Score: To be checked by the bank.
  • Acknowledgment Receipt: Provided by the bank upon submission of original documents.

Benefits of Loan Pre-Approval

Consider these pros and cons of getting pre-approved for a loan:

Advantages

  • Enhanced Negotiation Power: Knowing your budget helps in negotiating better terms.
  • Quicker Processing: Having a pre-approval can speed up the loan finalization process.
  • Bank Assistance: The bank can guide you through legal paperwork and project approvals, though personal due diligence is still recommended.
  • Simplified Procedure: Once pre-approved, only property documents need to be submitted for loan disbursement.

Disadvantages

  • Validity Constraints: Pre-approval is usually valid for 3 to 6 months, after which reapplication may be required.
  • Potential Project Approval Issues: Choosing an unapproved project could delay the process or necessitate finding an alternative.

Understanding Your Loan Offer Letter

Before signing the loan offer letter, ensure you understand these key terms:

Loan Amount: Confirm the amount sanctioned.
Interest Rate: Check the interest rate offered.
Type of Interest Rate: Determine if the rate is fixed, floating, or a combination.
Rate Adjustments: Be aware of any potential changes in the interest rate.
Disbursement Method: Confirm how and when the loan will be disbursed.
EMI Amount: Review the Equated Monthly Installment amount.
Loan Tenure: Know the duration of the loan.
Repayment Mode: Understand the method of repayment.
Special Offers: Look for any additional schemes or offers.
Terms and Conditions: Thoroughly review all terms and conditions.

Equated Monthly Installments (EMI)

EMIs comprise both principal and interest. Initially, a larger portion of the EMI goes towards interest, with the principal portion increasing over time. EMIs are generally calculated as a percentage of your net salary, known as the FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio). For instance, if your take-home salary is Rs. 1 lakh, you might be eligible for EMIs up to Rs. 60,000 for various loans.

Interest Rate Components

The interest on your loan includes the Base Rate plus a Spread.
Base Rate: The minimum rate set by the bank below which loans cannot be offered.
Spread: An additional rate set by the bank, varying based on loan duration, risk, and other factors. This can differ between banks, affecting your overall interest rate.
Although the RBI regulates the Base Rate, the Spread can change, impacting your EMI. Ensure you check for any potential increases in your EMI due to adjustments in the Base Rate or Spread.

Choosing a House

Selecting the Right Location

The real estate market in major metropolitan areas is becoming less favorable for investment due to high property prices and increasing demand for lower rates. It's wise to consider locations outside these major cities where property prices are more manageable. These emerging neighborhoods often offer larger homes at better prices and have potential for future growth due to improving infrastructure and connectivity. When looking for a home within a budget, it’s important to evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of different locations.


Site Visit Checklist

Ensure a thorough inspection during your site visit with the following checklist:

Document the Site: Visiting during less favorable conditions, such as heavy rain, can help identify potential issues.
Assess Construction Quality: Look for signs of cracks or moisture in the ceilings and walls.
Evaluate Interior Finishes: Check the quality of fixtures, including flooring and cabinets.
Inspect the Foundation: Look for cracks on the outer walls that might suggest poor construction.
Review Inspection Reports: Obtain the results of the most recent building inspection.
Consider Professional Assessment: Hiring a structural engineer can provide an in-depth evaluation of the building’s integrity.
Check Lift Performance: Evaluate waiting times for lifts during peak hours.
Verify Power Backup: Ensure there is a reliable emergency power supply.
Confirm Water Supply: Make sure the water supply is available around the clock.
Test Electrical Systems: Ensure all switches are operational.
Check Phone Signal: Test phone reception throughout the property.
Examine Plumbing: Flush toilets and run taps to check for good water pressure and plumbing conditions.
Look for Leaks: Inspect for leaks in walls, ceilings, and pipes.
Assess Flood Risk: Evaluate potential moisture damage and its impact on transit.
Review Security Measures: Confirm the presence of security features such as guards or cameras and check for any past security issues.
Observe Environmental Factors: Note how light and noise levels change throughout the day.

Negotiating

Effective negotiation is crucial. Here are some strategies to enhance your bargaining power:

Be Discreet: Avoid revealing too much about your personal situation or opinions about the property.
Ask Insightful Questions: Find out if the seller is in a hurry to sell and the level of interest from other potential buyers.
Identify Potential Issues: Look for any concerns that the agent might overlook or downplay.
Seek Expert Guidance: Consult with a real estate professional or an informed friend for valuable insights.
Do Your Research: Conduct your own research rather than relying solely on the agent's information.
Start with a Lower Offer: Begin negotiations with an amount below your maximum budget and be ready to negotiate.
Research Local Market: Speak with local residents to get an understanding of the area’s pricing.
Use Available Tools: Utilize resources such as Housing.com’s property valuation tool to assess market value and compare with nearby properties.

Handling Construction Delays

What steps can you take if there are delays in construction?

If a developer causes significant delays, buyers have several options:
Pursue Legal Action: Under the Consumer Protection Act, buyers can file a case against the developer. The court may require the developer to complete the project within a specified timeframe, with further legal actions possible for non-compliance.
File a Civil Lawsuit: Buyers can seek a refund of their investment through a civil lawsuit if the project is stalled or possession is delayed.

Assessing Developer Reputation

Here’s how you can evaluate a developer’s reputation:

Check Legal Credentials: Verify that the developer has the necessary legal qualifications and due diligence documents.
Review Track Record: Visit previous projects and inquire about the number of completed and ongoing developments.
Assess Timeliness: Confirm if past projects were completed on schedule.
Consult Online Forums: Look for feedback and reviews on real estate forums such as IREF.
Verify Affiliations: Check if the builder is associated with industry organizations like CREDAI or BAI.
Conduct Online Searches: Look for news articles and reviews about the builder and their projects online.
Check Financial Approval: Ensure the project is pre-approved by reputable financial institutions.

Hidden Costs and Taxes

Hidden Costs and Taxes in Home Buying

Purchasing a home involves considerable financial planning. In major cities like Mumbai, Bengaluru, Delhi-NCR, and other metro areas, the EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) for home loans is typically high due to elevated property prices. Ensure that you are financially prepared not only to handle the EMI but also to cover the margin money required. Being eligible for a loan and having sufficient financial resources are crucial for a successful home buying process.

Regular Expenses When Buying a Home

Monthly repayments consist of an EMI, which includes both principal (the borrowed amount) and interest (the cost charged by the bank). Initially, a larger portion of the EMI goes towards interest, but over time, the principal repayment increases. The structure of your repayment plan can affect your tax benefits, as different tax deductions are available for interest and principal repayments.

Taxes and Additional Costs
  • Stamp Duty: Generally 5% of the property value, with a penalty of 2% for each month of delay. For more details, visit Stamp Duty during Property Registration.
  • Registration: Approximately 1% of the property value, capped at Rs. 30,000. This should be completed within four months of the agreement, following payment of stamp duty. More information can be found at Laws Related to Registration of Property Transactions in India.
  • Brokerage: Typically 1% to 2% of the transaction value. For guidance, see Choosing a Licensed Broker.
  • VAT / Cess: VAT covers materials used for construction, while service tax applies to the sale value. Since April 1, 2015, service tax is 14% on a portion of the sale value; 3.5% for properties under Rs. 1 crore, and 4.2% for properties over Rs. 1 crore, though this varies by state. Parking and maintenance facilities also attract service tax. Single residential units like independent villas or bungalows are exempt. If a project is completed and all approvals, including an Occupancy Certificate (OC), are obtained before sale, no service tax or VAT is applicable.
  • TDS (Tax Deducted at Source): A 1% TDS on properties valued at Rs. 50 lakh or more is required from the buyer. This can rise to 20% if the seller does not provide their PAN details. For installment payments, TDS must be deducted with each installment. TDS is also applicable for under-construction properties purchased on or before June 1, 2013, for which outstanding payments are subject to TDS.

Second Home Buyers

What Should I Consider Before Buying a Second Home?
  • Verify the developer’s credentials and reputation by checking forums like IREF.
  • Ensure the project is approved by leading financial institutions and is eligible for financing.
  • Be prepared for potentially lower financing amounts on a second home, which might require a higher upfront payment.
  • Expect higher interest rates as lenders view the purchase as an investment.
  • Consider the impact of existing loans on the new loan amount you can secure.
  • Plan for potential financial issues by ensuring property investments do not exceed 50% of your total asset value.
  • Review tax implications specific to second homes, which differ from those of a first home.
  • Consider what makes the property appealing to renters, such as neighborhood amenities, connectivity, furnishing, and maintenance, which affect rental value and costs.
